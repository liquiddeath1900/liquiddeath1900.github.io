Critical Issues & Security Vulnerabilities

These issues are directly impacting your Google visibility and should be addressed first.

1. Google Business Profile Shows Lincoln, CA — Not Sacramento Critical

GBP Address: Lincoln, CA | Website Address: 8880 Elder Creek Road, Sacramento, CA 95828

Impact: Your Google Business Profile is registered to Lincoln, CA, but your website and actual service hub is Sacramento. This address mismatch confuses Google about where your business is located and severely hurts your local pack rankings for Sacramento searches. When someone searches "weed delivery Sacramento," Google sees your business as being in Lincoln — a different city 30 miles away.

Fix: Update your GBP address to Sacramento, CA (or set it as a Service Area Business centered on Sacramento). Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) is identical across your website, GBP, and all directories. This single fix could dramatically improve your Sacramento rankings.

2. No Sacramento Landing Page High

Missing: /service-areas/sacramento

Impact: Sacramento is your headquarters and primary market. You have 14 service area pages for surrounding cities but none for Sacramento itself. Anyone searching "weed delivery Sacramento" or "cannabis delivery Sacramento CA" won't find a dedicated, optimized page on your site. This is your single biggest missed opportunity.

Fix: Create /service-areas/sacramento with localized content covering neighborhoods (Midtown, East Sac, Land Park, Natomas, etc.), zip codes, and Sacramento-specific FAQs. Follow the same format as your Roseville page.

3. No OG Image — Broken Social Sharing High

og:image not set on any page

Impact: When someone shares your link on Instagram, iMessage, Facebook, or Twitter, there's no preview image. This makes shared links look unprofessional and dramatically reduces click-through rates. Every share is a missed marketing opportunity.

Fix: Create a 1200x630px branded OG image and add <meta property="og:image"> to your Next.js layout. Takes 30 minutes, instant impact on every social share.

4. Missing Security Headers High

Missing: Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, X-XSS-Protection

Impact: Your site is vulnerable to clickjacking (embedding in malicious iframes) and cross-site scripting attacks. For a cannabis business handling customer data and payments, this is a trust and compliance risk.

Fix: Add security headers in your Netlify config (netlify.toml or _headers file): Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options: DENY, X-XSS-Protection: 1; mode=block, Referrer-Policy: strict-origin-when-cross-origin.

5. Duplicate H1 Tags on Homepage Medium

Found 2 H1 tags: "Are you 21 or older?" (age gate) and "Premium Cannabis Delivery" (hero)

Impact: Google sees two main headings and gets confused about the page's primary topic. The age gate H1 dilutes the SEO value of your real heading.

Fix: Change the age gate heading to an H2 or a styled div. Keep "Premium Cannabis Delivery" as the only H1.

6. Blog Section is Empty Medium

/education/blog — only contains a "Hello world!" placeholder post

Impact: An empty blog signals to Google that the site isn't actively maintained. Blog content drives organic traffic for informational queries ("best edibles for sleep," "sativa vs indica for anxiety") that bring in new customers.

Fix: Publish 2-4 cannabis education articles per month. Target informational keywords that Sacramento cannabis consumers are searching for. Each article cross-links to your shop and service area pages.

7. No Nevada City Service Area Page Medium

Missing: /service-areas/nevada-city

Impact: You mentioned serving the Sacramento-to-Nevada City corridor, but there's no landing page for Nevada City, Grass Valley, or the broader Nevada County area. This is a less competitive market with high intent.

Fix: Create /service-areas/nevada-city covering Nevada City, Grass Valley, Penn Valley, and the Highway 49 corridor. Low competition = fast ranking potential.
