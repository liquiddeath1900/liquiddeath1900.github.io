High Severity Issues

These need immediate attention to protect your business and customers.

1. Missing Security Headers HIGH

What's happening: Your website is missing important security settings that protect against common attacks.

Missing Header What It Protects Against
Content-Security-Policy Hackers injecting malicious code into your site
X-Frame-Options Clickjacking (tricking users into clicking hidden buttons)
X-XSS-Protection Cross-site scripting attacks
Referrer-Policy Leaking sensitive URLs to other websites
How to fix: In Framer, go to Site Settings > Custom Code > Head and add security headers. Or use Cloudflare (free) to add them automatically.

2. Developer Email Exposed on App Store HIGH

What's happening: The Google Play Store shows personal email and phone numbers that hackers could use for phishing attacks.

Exposed Info Risk
william.shtainer@greenuprecycle.com Targeted phishing emails pretending to be Google/Apple
+1 347-551-4319 Social engineering phone calls
1109 Flushing Ave, Brooklyn Physical security concerns
How to fix: Update Play Store listing to use a generic email like developer@greenuprecycle.com. Consider using a business phone number instead of personal.

Medium Severity Issues

These create legal risk or make your business look unprofessional.

3. Privacy Policy Has Placeholder Text MEDIUM

What's happening: Your privacy policy looks unfinished, which could cause legal problems.

Problem Found Current Text
Incomplete date "Last modified: March ___, 2025"
Missing link "[URL TO TERMS OF USE]"
Wrong page title "Privacy policy - My Framer Site"
Empty CCPA metrics All fields show "[__]"
How to fix: Update privacy policy with correct dates, links, and company name. This is important for California users (CCPA compliance).

4. Dead Link on Website MEDIUM

What's happening: The "Start Earning" button links to a site that doesn't exist (greenup.coastart.studio).

How to fix: Update the button to link to your app store pages or remove it.

5. Two Different Business Addresses MEDIUM

What's happening: Your website and app store show different addresses, which confuses customers and hurts Google rankings.

Source Address
Play Store 1109 Flushing Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11237
Website Footer 65-55 Traffic Ave, Ridgewood, NY 11385
How to fix: Pick one address and use it everywhere - website, app stores, Google Business Profile.

6. Technology Stack Exposed MEDIUM

What's happening: Your website reveals it's built on Framer with exact version numbers. Hackers look for this info.

How to fix: This is mostly unavoidable with hosted platforms, but monitor Framer security updates and apply them quickly.
